I'm a 3rd-year UCSB student who started in Computer Science and recently shifted to Statistics & Data Science — a move that reflects who I actually am as a thinker.

I chose CS because I spent my childhood helping my immigrant parents navigate technology, wanting to build tools that make everyday life easier. But what excited me most wasn't writing software — it was analyzing data, uncovering insights, and using evidence to guide better decisions.

That shift led me to Data Science, where I blend engineering and analytics to build solutions that help people like my parents. Right now I work as a Data Journalist at the Daily Nexus, UCSB's student news network, analyzing large datasets and creating visualizations for investigative stories.
