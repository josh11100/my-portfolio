About

Hi, I'm Joshua — a 3rd year at UC Santa Barbara. I started in Computer Science, but recently made the move to Statistics and Data Science. That switch wasn't a detour — it reflects how my thinking has evolved.

Growing up, I spent my childhood helping my immigrant parents navigate technology — translating interfaces, troubleshooting devices, bridging the gap between them and a digital world that wasn't built with them in mind. That shaped why I got into tech: to build tools that make everyday life easier for real people.

But the deeper I went, the more I realized what excited me wasn't just writing software — it was analyzing data, uncovering insights, and using evidence to guide better decisions. Data Science lets me blend engineering and analytics to build solutions that actually help people like my parents.

Right now I work as a Data Journalist & Analyst at the Daily Nexus, UCSB's student news network, where I dig into large datasets and build visualizations that power investigative stories. I'm excited about opportunities where I can keep building reliable, data-driven systems that solve meaningful problems.
